Case narrative

From affordability anxiety to actionable levers

NYC’s affordability headlines lacked a borough-level signal recruiters could trust. I reframed the prompt into a structured journey — clarify the business pain, test hypotheses with data, and deliver a point of view executives can act on.

  1. 1 · Define the problem

    Interviewed housing policy notes and rental reports to scope the risk: mid-income renters were losing ground despite recovery.

  2. 2 · Engineer trustworthy data

    Automated borough-level extracts, normalized rent + ACS income, and stitched transit/environment markers for context.

  3. 3 · Model and pressure-test

    Ran YoY growth, regression, and correlation diagnostics to isolate which factors drove divergence.

  4. 4 · Communicate the story

    Designed scrollytelling, narrative HUD, and downloadable evidence so non-technical reviewers stay oriented.

Surfaced a borough rent spread widening by $1,150 since 2010 and quantified transit as explaining 38% of variance.

Codified an ETL-to-visualization pipeline with automated CSV syncs, semantic JSON summaries, and static deploy scripts.

Tech & data stack

Built with a PHP ETL backend, PostgreSQL + GeoJSON spatial layers, and D3.js inspiration rendered through Chart.js and bespoke canvas heatmaps for a performant static experience.

Engineering
